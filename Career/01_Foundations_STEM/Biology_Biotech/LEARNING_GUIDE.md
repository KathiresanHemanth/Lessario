## Learning Path

### Phase 1: Fundamentals (Week 1-4)
1. **Cell Biology** — Cell structure, organelles, DNA, RNA, proteins
2. **Genetics** — DNA replication, transcription, translation, mutations
3. **Molecular Biology** — Gene expression, regulation, epigenetics
4. **Biochemistry** — Amino acids, enzymes, metabolism, ATP
5. **Microbiology** — Bacteria, viruses, fungi, immune system

### Phase 2: Applied Biology (Week 5-10)
6. **Human Anatomy & Physiology** — Organ systems, nervous system, circulatory
7. **Neuroscience** — Neurons, synapses, neurotransmitters, brain regions
8. **Genetics & Genomics** — Genome sequencing, SNPs, GWAS, heredity
9. **Immunology** — Immune response, antibodies, vaccines, autoimmunity
10. **Ecology & Evolution** — Natural selection, ecosystems, biodiversity

### Phase 3: Biotechnology (Week 11-16)
11. **Genetic Engineering** — CRISPR-Cas9, gene editing, gene therapy
12. **Synthetic Biology** — Designing biological circuits, BioBricks
13. **Bioinformatics** — Sequence analysis, BLAST, phylogenetics, Python
14. **Protein Engineering** — AlphaFold, protein design, drug targets
15. **Bioprocess Engineering** — Fermentation, bioreactors, scale-up

### Phase 4: Frontier Biology (Week 17-24)
16. **Brain-Computer Interfaces** — EEG, ECoG, neural implants (Neuralink)
17. **Tissue Engineering** — 3D bioprinting, organoids, regenerative medicine
18. **Computational Biology** — Modeling, simulation, systems biology
19. **Astrobiology** — Life in extreme environments, Mars biology
20. **Longevity Science** — Aging, telomeres, senescence, anti-aging research

## Neuralink — Why Biology + Engineering = Future

### How Neuralink Works
```
Brain → Neurons fire electrical signals (action potentials)
     → N1 chip implanted in motor cortex
     → 1024 electrode threads (thinner than human hair)
     → Reads neural signals at microsecond precision
     → Wireless transmission to external device
     → AI decodes intent (move cursor, type, etc.)
     → Enables paralyzed people to control devices

Required Knowledge:
- Neuroscience (how neurons work)
- Electrical Engineering (electrode design)
- Materials Science (biocompatible materials)
- AI/ML (signal decoding)
- Robotics (surgical robot for implantation)
- Software (real-time signal processing)
```

### Neuralink Job Requirements (Junior Roles)
- Neuroscience or Biomedical Engineering degree
- C++ and Python programming
- Signal processing (EEG, neural data)
- Experience with microelectronics or MEMS
- Understanding of FDA regulatory process
