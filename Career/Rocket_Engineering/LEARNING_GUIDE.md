# Rocket Engineering - Learning Guide

## 🎯 Goal
Understand rocket science — propulsion, aerodynamics, orbital mechanics, and launch systems for space exploration.

---

## Prerequisites
✅ Physics — Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Fluid Dynamics
✅ Mathematics — Calculus, Differential Equations, Linear Algebra
✅ Programming — MATLAB, Python, C/C++
✅ Chemistry — Propellant chemistry, combustion

---

## Core Concepts (in order)

1. **Rocket Fundamentals** — Thrust, specific impulse, mass ratio
2. **Propulsion** — Solid, liquid, hybrid, electric propulsion
3. **Thermodynamics** — Combustion, nozzle design, exhaust velocity
4. **Aerodynamics** — Drag, lift, supersonic/hypersonic flow
5. **Orbital Mechanics** — Kepler's laws, orbits, Hohmann transfer
6. **Structural Design** — Tank design, stage separation, payload fairing
7. **Guidance & Navigation** — INS, GPS, trajectory optimization
8. **Control Systems** — Thrust vector control, attitude control
9. **Staging** — Multi-stage rockets, payload optimization
10. **Re-entry & Recovery** — Heat shields, parachutes, landing

## Key Equations

### Tsiolkovsky Rocket Equation
```
Δv = Isp × g₀ × ln(m₀ / mf)
where:
  Δv  = change in velocity
  Isp = specific impulse (seconds)
  g₀  = 9.81 m/s²
  m₀  = initial mass (with fuel)
  mf  = final mass (without fuel)
```

### Thrust
```
F = ṁ × Ve + (Pe - Pa) × Ae
where:
  ṁ  = mass flow rate
  Ve = exhaust velocity
  Pe = exit pressure
  Pa = ambient pressure
  Ae = exit area
```

### Orbital Velocity
```
v_orbital = √(GM / r)
v_escape  = √(2GM / r)
where:
  G = gravitational constant
  M = mass of planet
  r = orbital radius
```

## Tips
- Start with model rockets (OpenRocket simulator)
- Master thermodynamics and fluid mechanics
- Learn MATLAB for trajectory analysis
- Study ISRO missions (Chandrayaan, Gaganyaan, PSLV)
- Follow SpaceX, NASA for cutting-edge concepts
- Aerospace engineering degree is ideal but not mandatory
