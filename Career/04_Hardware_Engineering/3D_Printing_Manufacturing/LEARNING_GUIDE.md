## Core Concepts

1. **3D Printing Technologies** — FDM, SLA, SLS, DMLS, MJF
2. **CAD for 3D Printing** — Design for additive manufacturing (DfAM)
3. **Slicing Software** — Cura, PrusaSlicer, layer height, infill, supports
4. **Materials** — PLA, ABS, PETG, Nylon, TPU, metals (Ti, Al, Steel)
5. **CNC Machining** — G-code, milling, turning, toolpath generation
6. **Metrology** — 3D scanning, CMM, quality inspection
7. **Industry 4.0** — IoT + Manufacturing, smart factory, digital twin
8. **PCB Manufacturing** — From design to fabrication
9. **Injection Molding** — Mold design, tooling, mass production
10. **Robotics in Manufacturing** — Robot arms, pick-and-place, welding

## Tips
- **Buy a cheap 3D printer** — Creality Ender 3 (₹15K) or K1 (₹25K)
- **Learn Fusion 360** — free for students, does CAD + CAM + simulation
- **G-code is the language of manufacturing** — understand it
- **DfAM** (Design for Additive Manufacturing) is a key skill
- **Industry 4.0 = IoT + Manufacturing** — combine with your IoT skills
