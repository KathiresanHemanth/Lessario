# Weapon Manufacturing - Learning Guide

## 🎯 Goal
Understand weapon systems engineering — covering design, ballistics, materials, manufacturing, and safety systems for defense applications.

---

## Prerequisites
✅ Physics — Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Fluid Dynamics
✅ Materials Science — Metallurgy, composites, hardness
✅ Manufacturing — Machining, forging, casting, heat treatment
✅ Mathematics — Calculus, Differential Equations, Statistics

---

## Core Concepts (in order)

1. **Ballistics Fundamentals** — Internal, external, terminal ballistics
2. **Materials & Metallurgy** — Steel alloys, titanium, armor materials
3. **Mechanical Design** — Mechanisms, tolerances, stress analysis
4. **Propellants & Explosives** — Chemistry, burn rate, detonation
5. **Manufacturing Processes** — CNC machining, forging, rifling
6. **Guidance Systems** — INS, GPS, laser, infrared homing
7. **Fire Control Systems** — Targeting, tracking, computation
8. **Safety & Arming** — Safety mechanisms, arming sequences
9. **Testing & Qualification** — Proof testing, environmental testing
10. **Defense Standards** — MIL-STD, STANAG, BIS defense specs

---

## Key Concepts

### Internal Ballistics
```
Chamber Pressure → Propellant burn → Gas expansion → Projectile acceleration
Key: Maximum chamber pressure must not exceed barrel rating
```

### External Ballistics
```
Trajectory = f(velocity, angle, drag, gravity, wind)
Drag Force = 0.5 × Cd × ρ × v² × A
Range = v² × sin(2θ) / g  (simplified, no drag)
```

### Terminal Ballistics
```
Penetration depends on: kinetic energy, projectile design, target material
KE = 0.5 × m × v²
```
