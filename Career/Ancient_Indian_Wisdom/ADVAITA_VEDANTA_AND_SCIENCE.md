# PART 1: ADVAITA VEDANTA — The Crown Jewel of Indian Philosophy

## What is Advaita Vedanta?
```
ADVAITA = "Not Two" (Non-Dual)
VEDANTA = "End of the Vedas" (the ultimate conclusion)

Core teaching in ONE sentence:
"BRAHMAN alone is real. The world is appearance. 
 The individual self (Atman) IS Brahman."

— Adi Shankaracharya (788-820 CE)
  "Brahma Satyam, Jagat Mithya, Jivo Brahmaiva Na Aparah"

In simple words:
There is ONLY consciousness.
You, me, the tree, the star, the atom — all ONE consciousness
appearing as many.

Like the ocean and waves:
The wave thinks it's separate from the ocean.
But it IS the ocean. Always was. Always will be.
When the wave realizes this → liberation (Moksha).
```

```
The Einstein of spirituality.
```

## The Core Teachings

### 1. Brahman — The Absolute Reality
```
BRAHMAN is:
  - Infinite, formless consciousness
  - Not a "God" sitting somewhere — it IS everything
  - Sat-Chit-Ananda (Existence-Consciousness-Bliss)
  - Beyond time, space, causation
  - Cannot be described, only experienced

"That from which all beings are born,
 That by which they live,
 That into which they dissolve —
 THAT is Brahman."
 — Taittiriya Upanishad

Modern parallel: The quantum field.
  Physics says: Everything arises from a unified field of energy.
  Vedanta says: Everything arises from a unified field of consciousness.
  Same insight. Different language.
```

### 2. Atman — Your True Self
```
ATMAN = Your real identity. Not your name, body, mind, or emotions.
        Pure awareness itself.

"I am not the body. I am not the mind.
 I am the witness of both."

The body changes (child → adult → old)
The mind changes (happy → sad → angry → calm)
But the KNOWER of these changes doesn't change.
THAT unchanging awareness = Atman.

ATMAN = BRAHMAN
Your individual consciousness IS the universal consciousness.
The wave IS the ocean.
The drop IS the ocean.

"Tat Tvam Asi" — "THOU ART THAT"
(Chandogya Upanishad — one of the 4 Mahavakyas)
```

### 3. Maya — The Cosmic Illusion
```
If only Brahman exists, why do we see a world of multiplicity?

MAYA = The power of illusion that makes ONE appear as MANY.

The classic example:
  In dim light, you see a SNAKE on the ground. You're terrified.
  Someone brings a lamp. It was a ROPE all along.
  The snake was never real. But your fear was real.
  The rope didn't change. YOUR PERCEPTION changed.

  World = the snake (appears real, isn't ultimate reality)
  Brahman = the rope (was always the truth)
  Maya = the darkness (that caused the misperception)
  Enlightenment = the lamp (that reveals truth)

Maya is NOT saying "the world doesn't exist."
It's saying "the world doesn't exist THE WAY YOU THINK IT DOES."

The world exists as an APPEARANCE of Brahman.
Like a movie exists on a screen — but the screen is real, not the movie.
```

### 4. The Three States (Avastha Traya)
```
Shankara's brilliant analysis of consciousness:

WAKING (Jagrat):
  You perceive the world through senses.
  You have a body, name, identity.
  This feels "most real."

DREAMING (Svapna):
  You create an entire world inside your mind.
  In the dream, it feels 100% real.
  You have a body, you feel emotions, you experience events.
  Only when you wake up do you know it was "unreal."

DEEP SLEEP (Sushupti):
  No world. No body. No mind. No dreams.
  Yet YOU exist. Awareness continues.
  "I slept peacefully" = someone was aware of the peace.

QUESTION: In waking, you say dreams were unreal.
          What if from a HIGHER state, waking life is equally unreal?
          What is the CONSTANT across all three states?

ANSWER: Pure awareness (Atman).
        The witness of waking, dreaming, and deep sleep.
        That is your TRUE identity.

TURIYA (The Fourth):
  The state of pure consciousness itself.
  Not waking, not dreaming, not sleeping.
  The background awareness that makes all three possible.
  This is enlightenment — permanent Turiya.
```

### 5. The Five Sheaths (Pancha Kosha)
```
Like peeling an onion to find your true self:

Layer 5 (outermost): ANNAMAYA KOSHA — Physical body (food sheath)
  "I am this body" — WRONG. The body changes. You remain.

Layer 4: PRANAMAYA KOSHA — Energy/breath body
  Life force, prana, breath. Dies when body dies.

Layer 3: MANOMAYA KOSHA — Mental/emotional body
  Thoughts, emotions, desires. Constantly changing.

Layer 2: VIJNANAMAYA KOSHA — Intellect/wisdom body
  Discrimination, knowledge, understanding.

Layer 1 (innermost): ANANDAMAYA KOSHA — Bliss body
  The subtlest layer. Experienced in deep sleep, deep meditation.

BEYOND ALL 5: ATMAN — Pure awareness. Unchanging. Eternal.
  Not any of the sheaths. The one who WEARS them.

Practical exercise:
"I have a body, but I am not the body."
"I have emotions, but I am not the emotions."
"I have thoughts, but I am not the thoughts."
"I have knowledge, but I am not the knowledge."
"I have bliss, but I am not the bliss."
"I AM. Period. That is enough."
```

### 6. Self-Inquiry (Atma Vichara) — Ramana Maharshi's Method
```
Ramana Maharshi (1879-1950) — the greatest Advaita sage of modern times.
Attained enlightenment at age 16 without any teacher.
Spent 50+ years at Arunachala hill (Tiruvannamalai, Tamil Nadu).

His ONE teaching:
  Ask: "WHO AM I?"

Not as a philosophical question.
As a PRACTICE:

1. A thought arises (anger, desire, fear)
2. Ask: "To whom does this thought arise?"
3. Answer: "To me."
4. Ask: "WHO AM I?"
5. The mind turns inward, toward its SOURCE.
6. In that turning inward, the mind DISSOLVES.
7. What remains = pure awareness = Atman = Brahman.

"The mind will subside only by means of the inquiry 'Who am I?'
 The thought 'Who am I?' will destroy all other thoughts,
 and like the stick used for stirring the funeral pyre,
 it will itself be burned up in the end."
 — Ramana Maharshi
```

### The Mahavakyas (4 Great Sentences of Vedanta)
```
1. "Prajnanam Brahma" — "Consciousness is Brahman" (Aitareya Upanishad)
2. "Aham Brahmasmi"   — "I am Brahman" (Brihadaranyaka Upanishad)
3. "Tat Tvam Asi"     — "Thou art That" (Chandogya Upanishad)
4. "Ayam Atma Brahma" — "This Self is Brahman" (Mandukya Upanishad)

All four say the SAME thing in different words:
YOUR CONSCIOUSNESS = THE UNIVERSAL CONSCIOUSNESS
```
