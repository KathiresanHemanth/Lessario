### Mindset Shifts
```
Employee mindset:  "How do I get a raise?"
Founder mindset:   "How do I create value for millions?"

Employee mindset:  "That's not my job"
Musk mindset:      "I'll learn it and do it myself"

Employee mindset:  "It's too risky"
Musk mindset:      "The risk of NOT doing it is bigger"
```

### First Principles Thinking
```
Step 1: Identify the problem
Step 2: Break it down to fundamental truths
Step 3: Ignore how it's "always been done"
Step 4: Rebuild solution from ground up
Step 5: Execute relentlessly

Example (Tesla):
"Cars are expensive" → Why? → Parts, labor, dealer markup
→ Build own factory, sell direct, vertically integrate
→ Tesla costs less than BMW while being better
```

## Phase 1: Business Fundamentals (Week 5-12)

### Accounting & Finance Basics
- **Revenue** — Money coming in
- **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)** — Direct cost to make product
- **Gross Profit** = Revenue - COGS
- **Operating Expenses (OpEx)** — Salaries, rent, marketing
- **Net Profit** = Gross Profit - OpEx - Tax
- **Cash Flow** — Actual money in/out (≠ profit)
- **Runway** = Cash in bank / Monthly burn rate

### Key Financial Statements
| Statement | What It Shows |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Income Statement (P&L)** | Revenue, expenses, profit over time |
| **Balance Sheet** | Assets, liabilities, equity at a point |
| **Cash Flow Statement** | Where money comes from and goes |

### Business Models
| Model | Example | Revenue |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **SaaS** | Slack, Notion | Monthly subscription |
| **Marketplace** | Amazon, Uber | Commission on transactions |
| **Hardware** | Tesla, Apple | Product sales |
| **Freemium** | Spotify, Zoom | Free + paid tier |
| **Advertising** | Google, Meta | Ad revenue |
| **DTC (Direct)** | Tesla, Warby Parker | Direct to consumer |

## Phase 2: Startup Building (Week 13-24)

### The Startup Process
```
1. IDEA → Identify a real problem worth solving
2. VALIDATE → Talk to 50+ potential customers
3. MVP → Build minimum viable product (fastest possible)
4. LAUNCH → Get first 10 paying customers
5. ITERATE → Improve based on feedback
6. SCALE → Growth marketing, hiring, funding
7. RAISE → If needed, approach investors
```

## Phase 3: How Elon Musk Operates

### His Daily Operating Principles
1. **Work 80-100 hours/week** — Extreme output
2. **First principles on everything** — Never accept "that's how it's done"
3. **Vertical integration** — Build everything in-house (Tesla makes own seats, chips, software)
4. **Hire the best, fire the rest** — "A small group of A players beats a large group of B players"
5. **Set impossible deadlines** — Then deliver 70% of impossible (which is still amazing)
6. **Sleep on the factory floor** — Be closer to production than any CEO
7. **Read constantly** — He read the entire encyclopedia as a child, mastered rocket science from textbooks
8. **Question every requirement** — "The best part is no part. The best process is no process."
9. **Rapid iteration** — "If things are not failing, you are not innovating enough"
10. **Multi-planetary thinking** — Think 100 years ahead

### His Management Framework
```
Step 1: Question every requirement
Step 2: Delete any part/process you can
Step 3: Simplify and optimize (ONLY after step 2)
Step 4: Accelerate cycle time
Step 5: Automate (LAST step, not first)

"The most common error is to optimize something that shouldn't exist"
```

### How He Learns New Fields
```
1. Read the foundational textbooks (not pop science)
2. Talk to the world's top experts
3. Ask them questions until you understand first principles
4. Apply knowledge immediately in a project
5. Iterate and learn from failures fast

He learned rocket science, manufacturing, AI, tunneling this way
```

## Phase 4: Scaling & Leadership (Week 25+)

### Hiring
- Hire people **smarter than you** in every domain
- Culture fit matters as much as skills
- Give autonomy + accountability
- Pay equity (ESOPs) > high salary for early employees

### Marketing & Brand (Elon's Way)
- **Zero ad spend** — Tesla spends $0 on advertising
- **Product IS the marketing** — Build something so good people talk about it
- **Social media presence** — Elon's Twitter IS Tesla/SpaceX marketing
- **Mission-driven** — "Accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy"

### Scaling Principles
```
10 customers → Do things that don't scale (manual)
100 customers → Build processes
1,000 customers → Build systems
10,000 customers → Build teams
100,000+ customers → Build culture
```
